Juice and purée Brix varies with ripeness — measure yours rather than trusting averages. A ripe watermelon can range from 10–15°Bx.

Alcohol freezes at −114°C but depresses the mixture's freeze point significantly. Each % ABV in the final mix lowers the freeze point by roughly 0.4–0.5°C. The calculator accounts for this, plus the sugar each specific drink actually carries — a dry spirit like vodka and a sweet liqueur like Kahlúa can share an ABV but contribute very different amounts of sugar.

Acid (citric, tartaric) at 0.2–0.5% by weight has negligible effect on Brix but increases perceived sweetness — letting you reduce actual sugar by 1–2°Bx without a flat result.
